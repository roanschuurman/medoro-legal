Frequently asked questions

How do I get started?

After installing Medoro, the onboarding takes about a minute. You will pick a name, choose a colour scheme, optionally enable check-in reminders, and create your first Journey. A Journey represents one medication and the doses you take of it. Once your Journey is set up, you can do a daily check-in from the Home tab.

Where is my data stored?

On your device. Medoro has no servers, no accounts, and no cloud sync. Everything you log — moods, events, medications, journal notes — lives in a local database on your phone, encrypted at rest by the operating system. To verify this, you can enable Airplane Mode and use the app normally; everything still works because nothing leaves the device.

How do I back up my data?

Settings → Data Export. Tap "Export backup" to save a JSON file with everything in it (journeys, stages, mood logs, events, custom tags, reminders, menstrual cycles). You can save the file to iCloud Drive, Files, or any cloud provider you trust. To restore on a new device, install Medoro and use Settings → Data Import.

The medication I'm prescribed isn't in the list. What now?

Medoro ships with a curated database of around 74 medications across antidepressants, anxiolytics, ADHD treatments, mood stabilisers, and sleep aids. If yours isn't listed, choose "Add a custom medication" at the bottom of the search results. You can enter the name and dose yourself; pharmacokinetic features (concentration curves) won't be available for custom entries because we don't have half-life data, but everything else works the same.

What does the concentration curve actually show?

For medications with a documented half-life, Medoro estimates how the level of the medication in your body changes over time based on your schedule. This is an educational model, not a measurement. Individual pharmacokinetics vary based on metabolism, weight, other medications, and many other factors. Always consult your prescriber before making any decisions about your medication.

The 7-day free trial has ended. What happens?

After the trial, Medoro asks you to make a one-time purchase (currently US$4.99) to continue. There are no subscriptions, no recurring charges, and no premium tiers. If you do not purchase, your data stays on your device — you can resume it any time you decide to unlock the app.

How do I get a refund?

Refunds for App Store purchases are handled by Apple, not by Medoro. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, sign in with your Apple ID, and request a refund for the Medoro purchase.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Email hello@aivenya.com with as much detail as you can — what you were doing, what you expected to happen, what actually happened, and your device model and iOS version if you have them. Screenshots help. We read every message.

Is Medoro a medical device?

No. Medoro is a personal journal and tracking tool. It does not diagnose, treat, monitor, or provide clinical advice. Always consult your doctor or psychiatrist before making any changes to your medication.

Where can I find the privacy policy?

At roanschuurman.github.io/medoro-legal. The short version: we collect nothing.
